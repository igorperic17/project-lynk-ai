# Biodiversity discovery and phenology in Acadia National Park

Biodiversity discovery and phenology in Acadia National Park engages the public in recording observations of plants and animals in Acadia. Volunteers can participate through a variety of methods, including targeted bioblitzes, ranger-led or Schoodic Institute-led programs such as Sea Watch or Hawk Watch, or by submitting observations on their own through iNaturalist, eBird, or Nature&#39;s Notebook. The project is a partnership of the National Park Service and Schoodic Institute, and aims to improve understanding of natural resources, inform management decisions, and provide educational and inspirational experiences for park visitors and local communities.
